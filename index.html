Abstract

The garden has appealed to the imagination for centuries and has been a great source of inspiration for humanity. People tend to romanticize the garden, where its dark narrative is left out. In my thesis, I want to create awareness of the connection between colonialism and the garden through ornaments.

Ornaments are historical art and craft pieces which are made for human pleasure, but are also telling a history of oppression and wealth. Ornaments symbolize wealth and immortality. Nature is immortal, with its ability to keep transforming and adapting to human interference. The ecological cycle is about change, transformation, about the cycle of life and death. The interconnectedness of all animate and inanimate things in the world, makes us highly dependent on each other.

The disappearance of Dutch native plants is a result of the colonial past. The Western hierarchical system has emerged from a past of working on the plantations and the oppression of the gardeners working on them. As a result of European territorial expansion, besides people and animals, they also brought exotic plants and the knowledge of indigenous people back home. Through my research I am questioning if taxonomy—the act of naming, describing and classifying organisms, including all plants, animals and microorganisms of the world—can also lead to a botanical decolonization?

Chapter 1

The garden is a physical space associated with a sanctuary, a space where the cycle of life is expressed, a space for rest from daily hectic and worries, which is often referred to as paradise. The garden has appealed to the imagination for centuries, it is an aesthetic space, it is a romanticized space.

Sub-content

This piece of land changes every day. Not only due to the weather but also the inhabitants that move around the space, making use of the objects surrounding it. Eating, breathing, moving, living, smelling, relaxing, fighting, loving, and sleeping. A garden is an artificial space created by people… The garden is a space where everyone holds their own piece of nature, in their own structured way. It is an enclosed space where the owner, the gardener, takes care of life in an ordered way, of categorizing.

But not only do they need the environment, also the environment needs them. In order to live, the plants in the garden need inhabitants. Plants are not able to move around from place to place in order to reproduce oneself. They need inhabitants for that, which move around and breath (eat, breath and move around) in the environment. To live is to breath, not only for the animate world but also for the inanimate, which makes all of the inhabitants of the earth fully dependent on the atmosphere.
